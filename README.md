# dayshawn-cs250-final-project
Final Project for CS 250 Agile Development
Through this course‚ I learned more about how Agile and Scrum practices can be used to support the development of software applications․ Completing the Sprint Review and the Sprint Retrospective helped me consider the entire development process and figure out what went well and what could be improved․ That experience has taught me how structured collaboration and continuous feedback can assist in constructing effective software solutions․

When I translate user requirements into a program‚ I like to work in small steps․ User stories have helped me greatly‚ as they allow me to view the system from the perspective of the end user who uses that system․ Instead of just technical requirements‚ user stories help me understand what the user is trying to accomplish and why it matters‚ making it easier to design features that are actually useful and relevant․ User stories help me prioritize tasks and ensure that my development work aligns with my users' expectations for the application․

My approach to programming has changed in this course․ I tend to do short development cycles‚ trying to get a smaller solution working‚ rather than trying to solve the whole problem at once․ The Agile processes of sprint planning‚ daily standup‚ sprint review‚ and retrospectives‚ were vital to keeping the builds organized and adaptable for the future․ I plan to carry on with the Agile practices for the flexibility‚ nimbleness‚ and adaptability it provides to developers․ I'd like to take this opportunity to continue using this iterative development system‚ so I can test things as I go along rather than at the end of a project․

Being a good team player in software engineering means much more than just pulling your weight‚ but also includes good communication‚ taking responsibility‚ and willingness to allow others to help․ I learned in this course that good teams depend on openness and trust․ Being able to give and take criticism‚ helping others‚ remaining engaged‚ and contributing ideas while working to help the team solve problems makes for a good team member․ Thus‚ good teamwork in software development requires both technical skills and social skills․

This has been an educational project because I have been able to experience Agile and Scrum in a real-world environment Having this project to show in my portfolio shows that I am capable of working through the software development lifecycle, and I can work within an Agile environment․ I believe this will open more doors for me in the field of computer science․
